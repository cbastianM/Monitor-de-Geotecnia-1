Given the following data from a constant head permeability test, you are ask to compute for the hydraulic conductivity of the sample using the formula from eq. (5.15)

$$
k = \frac{QL}{Aht} \tag{1}
$$

**Paso 2**
2 de 4

Before substituting the given values, make sure that all units arte in uniform in accordance to what is required in the problem.

So,

$$
L = 460 \text{ mm} \times \frac{1 \text{ cm}}{10 \text{ mm}} = 46 \text{ mm}
$$

$$
h = 700 \text{ mm} \times \frac{1 \text{ cm}}{10 \text{ mm}} = 70 \text{ mm}
$$

$$
t = 3 \text{ mins} = 180 \text{ sec}
$$
**Paso 3**  
3 de 4

After converting the values, substitute now to the eq. (1) to obtain the unknown;

$$
k = \frac{QL}{Aht}
$$

$$
= \frac{354 \cdot 46}{22.6 \cdot 70 \cdot 180}
$$

$$
= 0.057 \, \frac{\text{cm}}{\text{sec}}
$$

**Resultado**  
4 de 4

$$
k = 0.057 \, \frac{\text{cm}}{\text{sec}}
$$
