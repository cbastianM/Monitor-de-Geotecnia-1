**Paso 1**  
1 de 4

At a given temperature the absolute permeability can be solve using the formula from eq. (5.11);

$$
k = \frac{\gamma_w}{\eta} \overline{K} \tag{1}
$$

---

**Paso 2**  
2 de 4

One factor affecting the result of the computation is the units of the given value, therefore, convert the given values to maintain the units in uniform;

$$
\eta = 1.005 \times 10^{-3} = 1.005 \times 10^{-7} \, \frac{\text{N} \cdot \text{sec}}{\text{cm}^2}
$$

$$
\gamma_w = 9.789 \, \frac{\text{kN}}{\text{m}^3} = 9.789 \times 10^{-3} \, \frac{\text{N}}{\text{cm}^3}
$$

---

**Paso 3**  
3 de 4

Since, the unknown is the absolute permeability $\overline{K}$ transform the formula eq.(1) to;

$$
\overline{K} = \frac{k \eta}{\gamma_w}
$$

So,

$$
\overline{K} = \frac{k \eta}{\gamma_w}
$$

$$
= \frac{0.832 \times 10^{-5} \cdot 1.005 \times 10^{-7}}{9.789 \times 10^{-3}}
$$

$$
= 8.54 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{cm}^2
$$

---

**Resultado**  
4 de 4

$$
\overline{K} = 8.54 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{cm}^2
$$
