**Paso 1**  
1 de 3

Using the values obtained from the constant head permeability test in the laboratory.

Computing for the head difference, using the formula from eq.(5.15);

$$
k = \frac{QL}{Aht}
$$

can be written as;

$$
h = \frac{QL}{AtK}
$$

so,

$$
h = \frac{QL}{AtK}
$$

$$
= \frac{120 \cdot 0.250}{0.014 \cdot 105 \cdot 60}
$$

$$
= 0.340 \text{ cm}
$$

$$
= 340 \text{ mm}
$$
**Paso 2**  
2 de 3

The value of the discharge velocity is also required in the problem, using the formula;

$$
Q = AV \tag{1}
$$

Combine the formula from eq. (5.6) and eq.(5.13),

$$
v = ki
$$

and,

$$
i = \frac{h}{L}
$$

So,

$$
v = k \left( \frac{h}{L} \right) \tag{2}
$$

Combine the formula of eq. (1) and eq. (2)

$$
Q = Ak \left( \frac{h}{L} \right)
$$

Compute for the discharge velocity,

$$
Q = Ak \left( \frac{h}{L} \right)
$$

$$
= 105 \cdot 0.014 \cdot \left( \frac{340}{250} \right)
$$

$$
= 1.992 \, \frac{\text{cm}^3}{\text{sec}}
$$
