### **Paso 2** 2 de 17

To determine each the dry unit weight of each moisture content, determine first the moist unit weight in each row, using the following relation:

$$\gamma_{moist} = \frac{W_{moist}}{V} \qquad \because (1)$$

Where the weight of the wet soil is denoted as $W_{moist}$, and the volume is denoted as $V$.


### **Paso 3** 3 de 17

Using Eq. (1), calculate the moist unit weight $\gamma_{moist}$ in each corresponding weight of the soil in the mold $W_{moist}$, given that the volume of proctor is $943 \text{ cm}^3$

For the $\gamma_{moist}$ when $W_{moist} = 14.5 \text{ N}$, we have:

$$\begin{aligned}

\gamma_{moist} &= \frac{W_{moist}}{V} \

&= \frac{\left(\frac{14.5}{1000}\right)}{\left(\frac{943}{100^3}\right)} \

&= 15.376 \text{ kN/m}^3

\end{aligned}$$

### **Paso 8** 8 de 17

Now, using the moist unit weight, and its corresponding moisture content, calculate the dry unit weight $\gamma_{dry}$ using the following relation:

$$\gamma_{dry} = \frac{\gamma_{moist}}{1 + w} \qquad \because (2)$$

### **Paso 9** 9 de 17
